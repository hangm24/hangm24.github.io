Reflecting on your practice is a key skill you need to develop over the course of your training year. This is a thought process that all effective practitioners follow after teaching lessons to enable them to identify what has worked and why and what didn't work quite as well and why. They then use this to inform their future planning.


What went well and why?

Identify positive aspects of the lesson, e.g. timings, pupil engagement, your own subject knowledge, pupil progress etc.



Impact on pupil progress and meeting the needs of all learners:

What did the pupils know at the end that they didn't know at the start? What could they do at the end that they couldn't do at the start? Could all children access the lesson? If so, why? If not, why?



What could you do differently? How would this promote pupil progress?

Consider teaching and learning activities, timings, resources and pupil groupings, behaviour management, etc.
